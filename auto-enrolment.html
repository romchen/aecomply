Everything you need to know

regarding automatic-enrolment, compliance and your duties as an employer

Auto-enrolment was introduced by the Government in 2012 as a means to encourage workers to save for their retirement at the earliest possible stage in their careers.

If you are an employer with staff working for you in the UK, then you are responsible for setting up the facilities to enable workers to invest those savings.

*Staging dates are generally based on the size of an employer’s PAYE scheme as at 1 April 2012. However, there are certain exceptions.

Your duties as an employer

From your staging date, you will need to automatically enrol certain workers into a qualifying workplace pension scheme and make (at least) a minimum level of contributions to the scheme.

However, there are a number of other employer duties you need to be aware of, including:

  • Categorising your workforce and monitoring their age and earning on an ongoing basis.
  • Communicating the correct information to workers (including statutory notifications).
  • Automatically enrolling (and re-enrolling) the relevant workers.
  • Dealing with those workers choosing to opt out of auto-enrolment as well as those who opt in.
  • Ensuring that the scheme chosen for auto-enrolment purposes meets the necessary criteria.
  • Make the required contributions to the pension scheme.
  • Keeping appropriate records in relation to actions taken.

Failure to comply with the employer duties can, in certain circumstances, lead to fines of up to £10,000 per day. æComplyLEX can help you satisfy these duties and avoid non-compliance.

Issues which can arise

Compliance with the new employer duties is not always straight forward.

Many employers will appreciate assistance in categorising their workforce, especially in considering whether their employer duties extend to those individuals who are understood to be agency workers, internationally mobile workers, personal service workers, seconded employees and individuals with non-standard employment arrangements.

Furthermore, employers may have questions in relation to the interaction of auto-enrolment with salary sacrifice or contractual enrolment.

Through a series of questions, æComplyLEX will be able to determine:

  • The pension arrangements (if any) currently used by your business and how such arrangements are structured.
  • An understanding of your workforce and the nature of the relationships which your business has with the individuals who work for you.

æComplyLEX will help you fulfil these duties and ensure you remain compliant.
