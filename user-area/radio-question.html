SMALL EMPLOYERS

Where, on 1st April 2012, an employer—

  • (a) had less than 50 workers; and
  • (b) had, or was part of, one or more PAYE schemes in which there were 50 or more persons,

That employer will be categorised as a “small employer” by the Pensions Regulator.

When assessing whether or not these criteria were met, it will be necessary to assess the relationships with the workforce as at 1 April 2012 and ensure that all workers are identified. Workers will include personal service workers as well as those working under a contract of employment.

These employers may have shared a PAYE scheme with another employer or they may have used the PAYE scheme to pay benefits to individuals who were no longer employed.

If such employers had not shared their PAYE scheme with another employer, or if they had not used the PAYE scheme to pay benefits to individuals who were no longer employed, they would likely have had a later staging date.

Therefore, there are special provisions which relate to small employers, allowing them to either:

  • (a) keep their original staging date; or
  • (b) move their staging to a later prescribed date.

MOVING YOUR STAGING DATE

If you are a small employer, the below table will show you (based on your original staging date) the prescribed date to which you could move your staging date.

When was your original staging date? You could move your staging date to:

  • 1 October 2012 and 1 November 2012 1 August 2015
  • 1 January 2013 and 1 February 2013 1 October 2015
  • 1 March 2013 and 1 April 2013 1 January 2016
  • 1 May 2013 and 1 June 2013 1 February 2016
